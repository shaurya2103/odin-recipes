Kadhai Paneer

Description

This classic, restaurant-style Kadai Paneer features a vibrant, spicy gravy with crunchy bell peppers and tender paneer, all tied together by a freshly roasted spice blend.

Ingredients

  • For the Kadhai Masala(Spice Blend)
  • 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 4-5 dry red chilies (adjust for spice)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • For the Gravy & Paneer:
  • 250 grams paneer (cubed)
  • 1 large capsicum (green bell pepper), cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large onion, cut into 1-inch cubes (layers separated)
  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee (divided)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
  • 1/2 teaspoon Kashmiri red chilli powder(for color)
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream or malai
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • ½ cup water (or as needed)
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped coriander leaves & ginger juliennes (for garnish)

Steps to Make Kadhai Paneer

  1. Make the Kadai Masala: In a dry pan, roast the coriander seeds, fennel seeds, dry red chilies, and cumin seeds on medium-low heat for 3–4 minutes until fragrant. Let them cool, then grind them into a semi-fine powder.
  2. Sauté the Veggies: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add the cubed onions and capsicum. Sauté on medium heat for 2-3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crunchy. Remove and set aside.
  3. Build the Gravy Base: In the same pan, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until translucent. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 30 seconds until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add Tomatoes & Spices: Add the chopped tomatoes (or puree) and cook until they become soft and start releasing oil from the sides. Stir in 1.5 tablespoons of the prepared kadai masala, Kashmiri red chili powder, and salt.
  5. Simmer the Gravy: Add ½ cup of water to the tomato-spice paste and let it simmer for 3-4 minutes to create a rich gravy.
  6. Combine Everything: Add the sautéed capsicum, onions, and paneer cubes to the gravy. Stir gently, cover, and let it cook on low heat for 2 minutes so the paneer absorbs the flavors.
  7. Finish the Dish: Sprinkle in the garam masala and crushed kasuri methi. Turn off the heat, stir in the heavy cream, and garnish with fresh coriander leaves and ginger juliennes.

    8. Serve hot with naan, roti, or paratha!
