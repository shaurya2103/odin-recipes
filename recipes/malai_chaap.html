Malai Chaap

Description

Malai Chaap is a popular North Indian vegetarian delicacy made from protein-rich soya chaap (a soy and wheat-based mock meat). The pieces are marinated in a rich blend of hung curd, fresh cream, and cashew paste, then grilled or roasted to achieve a melt-in-your-mouth, creamy, and smoky texture.

Ingredients

  • Main Ingredient: 500g soya chaap (sticks or canned)
  • The Creamy Marinade:
  • ½ cup fresh cream (malai) or heavy whipping cream
  • ½ cup thick curd (hung yogurt)
  • ¼ cup cashew nut paste (for richness)
  • Aromatics & Spices:
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1tsp green chili paste or finely chopped green chilies
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder or white pepper powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1tsp chaat masala (or chaap masala)
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Sautéing & Garnishing:
  • 2-3 tbsp butter or neutral cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion (diced into petals)
  • 1 capsicum (diced into cubes)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Freshly chopped coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Boil and Prep: Boil the soya chaap sticks in water with a pinch of salt and ginger-garlic paste for 5 minutes until soft. Remove, let them cool slightly, and pull out the wooden or ice cream sticks. Cut the chaap into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Fry the Chaap: Heat oil in a pan and shallow fry the chaap pieces on medium heat until they turn evenly golden brown.This gives a chewy texture. Remove and set aside to cool.
  3. Prepare the Marinade: In a large mixing bowl, combine the hung curd, fresh cream, cashew paste, grated cheese,ginger-garlic paste, and green chili. Mix thoroughly until smooth.
  4. Season: Add the black pepper, garam masala, salt, chaat masala, and kasuri methi to the yogurt and cream mixture.
  5. Marinate: Add the fried chaap pieces to the marinade. Toss to coat them thoroughly. Cover and leave to marinate in therefrigerator for at least 30 to 60 minutes.
  6. Cook or Grill:
    • Stovetop Pan-fry: Add 1 tablespoon of butter or oil to a pan. Cook the marinated chaap on medium heat until the marinade clings to the pieces and gets slight char marks (about 8–10 minutes).
    • Oven/Tandoor: Skewer the pieces and bake/grill at 180°C (350°F) for 15-20 minutes.
  7. Garnish & Serve: Toss with an extra splash of fresh cream, a squeeze of fresh lemon juice, and finely chopped coriander.Serve hot with mint chutney and onion rings.
