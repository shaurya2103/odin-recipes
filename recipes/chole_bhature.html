Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is one of the most popular North Indian dishes consisting of fluffy, deep-fried bhature served with a rich, spicy, tangy, and dark-colored chickpea curry (chole). This authentic restaurant-style recipe combines aromatic spices, perfectly cooked chickpeas, and soft bhature for a delicious and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • For the Chole (Chickpea Curry):
  • 1 cup chickpeas (Kabuli Chana), soaked overnight in 3 cups of water
  • Whole Spices for Boiling:
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tea bags
  • Aromatics:
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • Spices:
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1½ tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp chana masala
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Tang & Flavor:
  • 1 tsp dry mango powder (amchur) or pomegranate seed powder (anardana)
  • ½ tsp black salt
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Tempering & Cooking:
  • 2–3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

  • For the Bhatura:
  • Flours:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp fine semolina (sooji/rava)
  • Binder & Fats:
  • ¼ cup sour curd (yogurt)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Leavening Agents:
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Liquid:
  • Warm water (as needed for kneading)
  • Frying:
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Boil the Chickpeas: Drain the soaked chickpeas and add them to a pressure cooker with 2½ cups of water, black cardamom, green cardamoms, cinnamon, bay leaf, tea bags, salt, and a pinch of baking soda. Pressure cook for 8–10 whistles on medium heat until soft. Remove the tea bags and whole spices.
  2. Prepare the Masala: Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds and allow them to splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add Tomato & Spices: Mix in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for one minute. Add tomato puree, Kashmiri red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, chana masala, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Combine: Add the boiled chickpeas along with the cooking water. Mash a few chickpeas using the back of a spoon to make the gravy thicker.
  5. Simmer: Add amchur or anardana powder, black salt, and crushed Kasuri Methi. Simmer for 10–15 minutes on low heat. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and ginger juliennes.
  6. Prepare the Bhatura Dough: In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, sugar, salt, baking soda, and baking powder.
  7. Knead the Dough: Add curd and oil, then gradually pour in warm water and knead for 8–10 minutes until a smooth, soft, and slightly sticky dough forms.
  8. Rest the Dough: Grease the dough with a little oil, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest in a warm place for at least 2 hours.
  9. Roll the Bhature: Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Apply a little oil and roll each ball into an oval or oblong shape about ¼-inch thick.
  10. Fry: Heat enough oil in a deep kadai until very hot. Gently slide the rolled bhatura into the oil.
  11. Puff: Using a slotted spoon, gently press the bhatura and splash hot oil over the top until it puffs completely.
  12. Flip: Turn the bhatura over and fry for another 15–20 seconds until light golden. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  13. Serve: Serve the hot, fluffy bhature with rich chole, sliced onions, lemon wedges, and green chilies for an authentic restaurant-style meal.
